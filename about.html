Discover our heritage, spiritual traditions, and the sacred practices established at Krishnamath Sabha Bhavan, Belagavi.

Our Heritage

A Temple Steeped in Divine History

Udupi Krishna Math was established in 1285 AD by Jagadguru Sri Madhvacharya (Anandatirtha) — the revered philosopher-saint who propounded the Dvaita Vedanta (dualistic) school of philosophy. Sri Madhvacharya is said to have discovered the sacred idol of Lord Bala Krishna from a ship’s ballast and consecrated it here, marking the founding of this divine sanctuary.

Located in Ranade Colony, Hindwadi, Belagavi, Karnataka, Krishnamath Sabha Bhavan stands as a vibrant centre of Vaishnava devotion and community service — carrying forward the sacred traditions of Lord Sri Krishna’s worship for all devotees who seek divine blessings.

Over seven centuries, the Math has been lovingly maintained and expanded by successive generations of sanyasi heads (swamijis), preserving its original spiritual sanctity while welcoming millions of devotees from across India and the world.

Sacred Architecture

A Masterpiece of Dravidian Architecture

Udupi Krishna Math is a magnificent example of Kerala-Karnataka Dravidian architecture, featuring a gilded Garuda Kambha (Garuda pillar) at the entrance, intricately carved gopuras (tower gateways), and a sacred tank (Madhva Sarovara) within the temple premises.

The main sanctum houses the original idol of Lord Bala Krishna — installed by Sri Madhvacharya himself — visible to devotees through the famous Kanakana Kindi, a small window with nine holes crafted in a conch-shell lattice, associated with the miraculous story of Haridasa saint Sri Kanakadasa.

The temple complex includes the main shrine, the sacred Madhva Sarovara tank, pathashala buildings for Vedic education, a pilgrims’ dharamshala, Udupi’s renowned “Brahmin hotels” serving traditional meals, and beautifully maintained gardens.

Presiding Deities

The Divine Residents

The Math is blessed with the divine presence of Lord Sri Krishna and presiding figures of Madhva philosophy, each central to the Dvaita Vedanta tradition.

🦚

Lord Bala Krishna

The presiding deity of Udupi Krishna Math — a rare and beautiful depiction of Lord Krishna as an infant, holding a churning rod and rope. The original idol was discovered and consecrated by Sri Madhvacharya himself in 1285 AD. Devotees view the deity through the sacred Kanakana Kindi window.

🌸

Sri Madhvacharya

The revered founder of the Math and propounder of Dvaita Vedanta philosophy. Born in 1238 AD in Pajaka near Udupi, Sri Madhvacharya is one of the three great Vedantic acharyas of India. His portrait and teachings are venerated throughout the Math complex.

⚡

Sri Garuda

The divine vehicle of Lord Vishnu, revered in the Madhva tradition as a great devotee and attendant of the Lord. The golden Garuda Kambha (Garuda pillar) at the temple entrance is one of its most iconic features, symbolising devotion and swiftness to the Lord.

Our Journey

Temple Timeline

A chronicle of significant milestones in the sacred history of Udupi Krishna Math.

1285

Math Founded by Sri Madhvacharya

Jagadguru Sri Madhvacharya consecrates the idol of Lord Bala Krishna at Udupi, establishing the Math and the sacred Ashtamatha (eight mathas) tradition for its perpetual service.

~1500s

Kanakadasa’s Miracle — Kanakana Kindi

The Haridasa saint Sri Kanakadasa, refused entry due to caste discrimination, sang devotional songs outside. Lord Krishna, moved by his devotion, caused the western wall to crack open — creating the sacred Kanakana Kindi window still venerated today.

1600s

Paryaya System Formalised

The biennial Paryaya ceremony — in which one of the eight mathas takes charge of the temple every two years — is formally established as an unbroken tradition continuing to this day.

1800s

Major Renovations

The temple complex undergoes major architectural renovations, adding the Garuda Kambha, expanded gopuras, the Madhva Sarovara tank, and improved pilgrims’ facilities while preserving the original sanctum.

1950

Modern Administration

A formal trust structure is established to manage the Math’s affairs, vedic education, and charitable activities — expanding the free annadanam (meal service) to hundreds of pilgrims daily.

2020

Digital Outreach

Live streaming of daily aarti, poojas, and Paryaya ceremonies begins, allowing devotees worldwide to receive Lord Krishna’s blessings from their homes.

Why Visit Us

Spiritual Significance

Udupi Krishna Math is more than a place of worship — it is a living embodiment of Dvaita Vedanta philosophy and unbroken devotional tradition since 1285 AD.

Vedic Traditions

All poojas and sevas are performed strictly following ancient Vedic Pancharatra Agama traditions, maintaining the sanctity established over 200 years ago.

Vedic Education

The temple runs a Vedic school (pathashala) teaching Sanskrit, Vedic chanting, and traditional scriptures to children from the community free of charge.

Community Service

Daily Annadanam (free meal distribution) serves hundreds of pilgrims, the poor, and the underprivileged — an unbroken tradition since the temple's founding.
