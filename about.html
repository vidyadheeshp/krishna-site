Our Heritage

A Temple Steeped in Divine History

Sri Krishna Mandir was founded in 1812 by the revered saint Pandit Gopal Das Ji Maharaj, who received divine inspiration through a dream in which Lord Krishna himself appeared and commanded the establishment of this holy sanctuary.

Built on the sacred soil of Vrindavan — the land where Lord Krishna spent his childhood — the temple has been a centre of Vaishnava worship, Vedic scholarship, and spiritual refuge for devotees from across India and the world.

Over two centuries, the temple has been lovingly restored and expanded by successive generations of devoted trustees, maintaining its original spiritual sanctity while welcoming the needs of modern pilgrims.

Sacred Architecture

A Masterpiece of Temple Architecture

The temple is a stunning example of North Indian Nagara style architecture, featuring a soaring shikhara (spire) adorned with intricate carvings of divine beings, floral motifs, and scenes from the Bhagavata Purana.

The main sanctum houses a magnificent idol of Lord Sri Krishna in His flute-playing pose, flanked by Srimati Radharani and the eight principal gopis. The idol was consecrated by Jagadguru Shankaracharya in 1820.

The temple complex spans 3 acres and includes the main temple, a meditation hall, a Vedic school, a dharamshala for pilgrims, and beautifully maintained gardens with fragrant Tulsi plants and sacred Kadamba trees.

Presiding Deities

The Divine Residents

The temple is blessed with the divine presence of three principal deities, each representing a different aspect of the Supreme.

🦚

Sri Krishna

The principal deity of the temple — the eternal flute player, divine lover of Vrindavan, and the Supreme Personality of Godhead. His idol stands 4 feet tall, crafted from black marble, adorned daily with fresh flowers and gold ornaments.

🌸

Srimati Radharani

The eternal consort of Lord Krishna and the embodiment of divine love and devotion. Radharani's idol, carved from white marble, radiates an ethereal beauty that fills every devotee's heart with pure love.

⚡

Sri Balarama

Lord Krishna's elder brother and the embodiment of strength, protection, and dharma. Sri Balarama's presence in the temple ensures the protection and well-being of all devotees who seek His blessings.

Our Journey

Temple Timeline

A chronicle of significant milestones in the sacred history of Sri Krishna Mandir.

1812

Temple Founded

Pandit Gopal Das Ji Maharaj establishes the original shrine under a Kadamba tree at the present site, following divine revelation.

1820

Idol Consecration

The magnificent marble idol of Sri Krishna is installed by Jagadguru Shankaracharya in a grand 10-day Vedic ceremony attended by thousands.

1875

Main Temple Constructed

The present main temple structure with its 60-foot Nagara-style shikhara is completed by the patronage of Maharaja Scindia of Gwalior.

1950

Trust Established

Sri Krishna Mandir Trust is formally registered to manage and preserve the temple for future generations, expanding services to include free food distribution.

1990

Major Renovation

A comprehensive renovation preserves the original architecture while improving facilities for the growing number of pilgrims and devotees.

2020

Digital Outreach

Live streaming of daily aarti and poojas begins, allowing devotees worldwide to receive Lord Krishna's blessings from their homes.

Why Visit Us

Spiritual Significance

Sri Krishna Mandir is more than a place of worship — it is a living embodiment of Vrindavan's eternal spiritual energy.

Vedic Traditions

All poojas and sevas are performed strictly following ancient Vedic Pancharatra Agama traditions, maintaining the sanctity established over 200 years ago.

Vedic Education

The temple runs a Vedic school (pathashala) teaching Sanskrit, Vedic chanting, and traditional scriptures to children from the community free of charge.

Community Service

Daily Annadanam (free meal distribution) serves hundreds of pilgrims, the poor, and the underprivileged — an unbroken tradition since the temple's founding.
