Paryaya Mahotsav 2026 — Biennial Accession Ceremony

The sacred Paryaya is one of the most ancient and revered traditions of Udupi Krishna Math — a biennial ceremony in which one of the eight Ashtamathas takes charge of administering the temple. This grand event draws hundreds of thousands of devotees and scholars from across the world, and marks a new chapter of devotional service to Lord Krishna.

August 16, 2026 5:00 AM – 12:00 midnight+ Main Temple Grounds Free Entry

Janmashtami 2026 — Midnight Celebration

The grand celebration of Lord Bala Krishna’s divine birth with special poojas from dawn, culminating in the spectacular midnight aarti marking the exact moment of His birth. The Math is magnificently decorated, and special Udupi-style prasad is distributed to all devotees throughout the day.

August 28, 2026 5:30 AM – 9:00 PM Radha Shrine Free Entry

Festival — 9 Days

Madhvanavami — Jayanti of Sri Madhvacharya

A sacred celebration honouring the birth anniversary of Jagadguru Sri Madhvacharya, the founder of Udupi Krishna Math and propounder of Dvaita Vedanta philosophy. Special poojas, Vedic discourses on his philosophy, and processions mark this important occasion.

Oct 2 – Oct 10, 2026 6:00 PM daily Temple Auditorium Free Entry

Festival

Govardhan Puja & Annakut Mahotsav

The magnificent Annakut display of 108 food offerings to Lord Krishna followed by free Prasad distribution to all devotees. Udupi’s famous cuisine traditions make this an especially vibrant occasion — witness the spectacular mountain of food prepared by hundreds of devoted volunteers.

October 21, 2026 8:00 AM – 4:00 PM Temple Grounds Free Prasad

Spiritual Discourse

Srimad Bhagavatam 7-Day Katha

A week-long recitation and discourse on Srimad Bhagavatam by renowned Dvaita Vedanta scholar following Madhvacharya’s tradition. Explore the divine pastimes of Lord Krishna and receive spiritual wisdom grounded in the Tattvavada philosophy.

Nov 1 – Nov 7, 2026 10:00 AM – 1:00 PM Temple Hall Free Entry

Diwali Deepotsav 2026

Celebrate the festival of lights with a breathtaking display of 10,000 diyas (oil lamps) illuminating the entire temple complex. Special Lakshmi puja, fireworks, and a grand evening aarti mark this joyous occasion.
